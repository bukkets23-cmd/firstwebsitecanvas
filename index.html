The Invincibles · 2003 / 04

Unbeaten.
Unmatched.

In the 2003–04 Premier League season, Arsenal completed the entire 38-game campaign without a single defeat — a record that has never been replicated in the English top flight. The run extended to 49 consecutive unbeaten league games across two seasons. Forever Invincible.

Club History

Greatest Moments

1886

Born in Woolwich

Founded by workers at the Royal Arsenal munitions factory in Woolwich, south-east London. The club that would become the most decorated in FA Cup history began life among labourers who simply wanted a game of football.

1971

The First Double

Under Bertie Mee, Arsenal claimed the First Division title and FA Cup in the same season — the club's first League and Cup Double, sealing North London's grip on English football at Wembley.

1989

Thomas at Anfield

Needing to win by two goals at Anfield on the final day, Michael Thomas scored in injury time to snatch the First Division title from Liverpool. Brian Moore's call — "It's up for grabs now!" — echoes through football history.

1998

Wenger's First Double

In only his second full season, Arsène Wenger delivered a League and Cup Double. A new style of football — fast, technical, continental — had arrived at Highbury and English football would never be the same again.

2003 / 04

The Invincibles

Thirty-eight games. Twenty-six wins. Twelve draws. Zero defeats. Arsenal completed the entire Premier League season unbeaten — a record that has never been matched in English top-flight history.

2005

Vieira's Final Act

Patrick Vieira's last kick in an Arsenal shirt was the winning penalty against Manchester United in the FA Cup final at Cardiff. Thundered into the net, arm raised — a fitting farewell for the club's greatest captain.

2025

Title Restored

After a 21-year wait, Mikel Arteta's Arsenal lifted the Premier League trophy. Built through patience, belief, and an unshakeable identity — the title returned to the Emirates and a new dynasty announced itself to the world.

The greatest manager in Arsenal's history by any measure. Arriving from Nagoya Grampus Eight in 1996, Wenger revolutionised English football with a continental approach to diet, fitness, and possession-based attacking play. He gave us the Invincibles, the Highbury farewell, Thierry Henry, Patrick Vieira, and 22 years of an identity that will never fade. There will never be another.

PFA Player

Arrived from Juventus in 1999, Henry spent eight seasons at Highbury transforming from a struggling winger into the most prolific scorer in Arsenal history. Twice PFA Player of the Year, four-time Premier League top scorer, and the heartbeat of the Invincible season. His statue stands outside the Emirates — arm raised, shirt pulled, forever a Gunner.
